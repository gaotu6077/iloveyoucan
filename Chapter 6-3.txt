What to know about the measles outbreak in West Texas as cases rise to 90 - PBS NewsHour

A deadly measles outbreak has gripped West Texas. The state has seen a rise in measles cases in the past year and a half, officials said on Wednesday.

The outbreak is not the first outbreak in Texas that has affected the region.

Last year, a measles outbreak killed more than 9,000 people in the state.

In this year's outbreak, the number of cases in West Texas jumped by nearly 18 percent, officials said.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reported that two of the five measles cases were in the city of Dallas, which is in the central part of the state.

"We are now seeing a lot of cases, and it's not surprising that this is a huge outbreak," said Texas Health Presbyterian Hospital Director James L. Moore.

"For years, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention has been telling us that this outbreak is not very big, but we don't have the numbers that are necessary to make sure that that's true."
